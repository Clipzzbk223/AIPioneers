John McCarthy

The Father of Artificial Intelligence

An interactive presentation on his life and legacy.

Early Life & Education

Prodigy: Showed exceptional talent in mathematics from a young age, teaching himself college-level calculus as a teen.

Education: Earned a B.S. in Mathematics from Caltech (1948) and a Ph.D. from Princeton University (1951).

Early Interest: Began exploring the concept of "thinking machines" during his time at Princeton, laying the groundwork for his future career.

Major Contributions

LISP Programming Language (1958)

The first high-level language for AI research. It introduced groundbreaking concepts like recursion and automatic garbage collection. LISP's ability to treat code as data made it perfect for symbolic manipulation.

Time-Sharing Systems (1959)

Proposed allowing multiple users to access a single powerful computer simultaneously. This revolutionary idea was the direct ancestor of modern operating systems and cloud computing.

The Father of "Artificial Intelligence"

Coined the Term: He was the first to use the phrase "artificial intelligence" in his 1955 proposal for a research workshop.

The Birth of a Field: The subsequent 1956 Dartmouth Workshop, which he organized, is widely considered the event that officially launched AI as its own discipline.

Logic-Based Approach: Advocated for using formal logic to represent knowledge, a key school of thought in early AI research.

Influence on Modern AI

  • Programming Languages: LISP's functional programming concepts are alive in modern languages like Python and Clojure, which are staples in machine learning.
  • Cloud Computing: Time-sharing laid the conceptual foundation for the massive, shared computing infrastructure that powers today's large AI models (like ChatGPT and Gemini).
  • Explainable AI (XAI): His focus on logic and knowledge representation is still relevant in the push to create AI systems that can explain their reasoning.
